Overcoming MS

I am loving this organization and am feeling noticeably better following their recommendations. Find out more here.
“Overcoming MS (OMS) educates, supports and empowers people with MS in evidence-based lifestyle and medication choices that improve health outcomes. OMS is a global nonprofit organization that is registered as a charity in the USA, UK and Australia.”
“The OMS Recovery Program has one core mission: to help people with MS live longer, better and have healthier lives. Through a series of diet and lifestyle changes – all based on rigorous scientific research and evidence – we believe people with MS can make a dramatically positive impact on their quality of life.”
“The program consists of the following: diet, exercise, stress management, sunlight, vitamin D and omega-3 supplementation, and, when needed, medication.”
Enjoy

  • Vegetables
  • Fruits
  • Grains (pastas, rice, wheat, oats, corn, barley, etc)
  • Seeds and nuts
  • Beans, peas and other legumes
  • Fish and seafood
  • Dairy alternatives made from soy, rice, almonds, oats, etc
  • Egg whites
Avoid
  • Dairy (milk, cream, butter, ice cream, cheese, yoghurt, whey powder)
  • Baked foods, unless fat-free (commercial baked goods, prepared mixes)
  • Snacks (chips, corn chips, party foods)
  • Fats (margarine, shortening, mayonnaise, lard, coconut oil, palm oil, unnamed vegetable oils which are usually palm oil)
  • Meat (all land animals, including cured, processed or canned meats)
  • Fried and deep-fried foods (except those fried without oil, using water, wine or sauces like oyster or sweet chili sauce)
  • Fast food (burgers, fried chicken, fries, etc)
  • Egg yolks
